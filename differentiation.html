Differentiation (Derivative)

Differentiation (Derivative) is one of the most fundamental concepts of Calculus. It is widely used in Mathematics, Physics, Engineering, and Technical Subjects.

After learning Differentiation and Integration, students can understand many advanced concepts in Physics and Engineering.

Historical Background

Two great mathematicians independently developed Calculus:

  • Sir Isaac Newton
  • Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

Both made significant contributions to the development of Differentiation and Integral Calculus.

What is Differentiation?

Differentiation is the process of finding the rate at which one quantity changes with respect to another quantity.

If

y = f(x)

then

dydx

is called the Derivative of y with respect to x.

Meaning of Different Symbols

Symbol Meaning
dydx Derivative of y with respect to x
dxdy Derivative of x with respect to y
ddx Differentiation operator
dy Very small change in y
dx Very small change in x
Δy Finite (considerable) change in y
Δx Finite (considerable) change in x

Important Relation

ddx = 1dxdy

Similarly,

dxdy = 1dydx

These are reciprocal relationships (where applicable).

Very Small Change vs Finite Change

Very Small Change

  • dx → Infinitesimally small change in x
  • dy → Infinitesimally small change in y

Finite Change

  • Δx → Noticeable change in x
  • Δy → Noticeable change in y

Three Meanings of Derivative

1. Geometrical Meaning

Definition

The derivative represents the Slope of the Tangent to the curve at a particular point.

If

y = f(x)

then

dydx

is the slope of the tangent drawn at that point.

Important Point

2. Physical Meaning

Derivative represents the Instantaneous Rate of Change.

Whenever Physics asks:

  • Rate of change
  • Instantaneous rate
  • Change with respect to time

Differentiation is used.

Example

Suppose a water tank is draining.

The water level decreases continuously.

If the question asks:
At this instant, how fast is the water level decreasing?

Then dhdt gives the Instantaneous Rate of Change of Height.

Examples in Physics

Quantity Derivative
Velocityv = dxdt
Accelerationa = dvdt
Current changedIdt

Physical Meaning = Instantaneous Rate of Change

Mathematically, derivative means:

Finding differentiation using standard formulas.

Differentiation Rules (Properties)

1. Addition & Subtraction Rule

If

y = u ± v

then

dydx = dudx ± dvdx

Example

sin x + ex

Derivative: cos x + ex

Example

sin x - ex

Derivative: cos x - ex

2. Product Rule (Multiplication Rule)

If

y = u · v

then

dydx = u dvdx + v dudx

Example

x2ex

Derivative: x2ex + 2xex

Factorizing, ex(x2 + 2x)

Example

sin x · 2x

Derivative: 2xcos x + 2x(ln 2)sin x

3. Quotient Rule

If

y = uv

then

dydx = v dudx - u dvdxv2

Flowchart

4. Chain Rule

Used when one function is inside another.

General formula:

ddx [f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) × g'(x)

Example

sin(x3)

  • Step 1: Differentiate outer function → cos(x3)
  • Step 2: Multiply by derivative of inner function → 3x2

Answer: 3x2 cos(x3)

Example

cos(x4)

Derivative: -4x3 sin(x4)

Constant Multiplication Rule

If

y = 5f(x)

then

dydx = 5 dfdx

Constant remains unchanged.

Important Formula Classification (Mind Map)

Important Points

  • dy/dx means derivative of y with respect to x.
  • Geometrically, derivative gives the slope of the tangent.
  • Physically, derivative gives the instantaneous rate of change.
  • Mathematically, derivative is calculated using differentiation formulas.
  • Learn all basic formulas before solving problems.
  • Understand which rule (Addition, Product, Quotient, Chain) applies to each expression.

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing ax with xa.
  • Forgetting the ln a term in ax.
  • Forgetting to differentiate the inner function in the Chain Rule.
  • Missing the minus sign in derivatives of cos x, cot x, and csc x.
  • Applying Product Rule instead of Quotient Rule (or vice versa).

Memory Tricks

  • SIN → COS
  • COS → −SIN
  • TAN → SEC²
  • Constant → 0
  • Power Rule: Bring the power in front, then reduce it by 1.
  • Chain Rule: Outer derivative × Inner derivative.

Exam-Oriented Notes

  • Memorize all standard differentiation formulas.
  • Practice identifying the correct differentiation rule.
  • Always simplify expressions before differentiating, if possible.
  • In Physics, derivatives are frequently used to calculate velocity, acceleration, current change, temperature change, and other rates of change.

Quick Revision
